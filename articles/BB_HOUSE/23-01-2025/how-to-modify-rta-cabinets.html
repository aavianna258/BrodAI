2. Preparing to Modify Your RTA Cabinets

A successful upgrade begins with proper preparation. Here’s what you need to do before diving into how to modify RTA cabinets:

A measuring tape is a versatile tool that helps you measure the length, width, and height of the space where you want to install or modify your cabinets.

  • Gather Your Tools: Have screwdrivers, a power drill, measuring tape, sandpaper, primer, paint/stain, new hardware, and any accessories (pull-out trays, crown molding, etc.) ready.
  • Set Up a Workspace: Clear a table or counter where you can safely remove cabinet doors and store hardware. Use drop cloths to protect floors.
  • Plan Your Design: Decide on the color, finishes, and any added features like under-cabinet lighting or decorative trim. For design inspiration, you can browse HGTV’s Kitchen Ideas or Better Homes & Gardens .

Label each removed part (like hinges and doors) to make reassembly simpler. This small step saves a lot of time when learning how to modify RTA cabinets.

3. Step-by-Step: How to Modify RTA Cabinets

You can assemble or modify your RTA cabinet with basic tools—just follow the instructions carefully.

  1. Remove Existing Hardware:
    Unscrew cabinet doors, knobs, and hinges. Keep all screws in labeled bags. This precaution helps maintain order, an essential first step in how to modify RTA cabinets.
  2. Sand & Prep Surfaces:
    Lightly sand the cabinet frames and doors to remove any glossy finishes. Wipe away dust with a damp cloth to ensure a smooth base for painting or staining.
  3. Apply Primer & Paint or Stain:
    Use a primer if needed (especially if switching from dark to light colors). Once it’s dry, apply paint or stain in thin, even coats. Allow each coat to dry thoroughly before adding another.
  4. Upgrade the Hardware:
    Swap out old knobs for modern pulls or install soft-close hinges for a premium feel. Double-check your measurements to prevent crooked placement.
  5. Enhance Storage:
    Adding pull-out shelves, spice racks, or drawer organizers can revolutionize your cabinet functionality. Incorporating these elements is a major part of how to modify RTA cabinets for daily convenience.
  6. Add Decorative Moldings:
    Whether it’s crown molding on top or light rail molding beneath, trim can elevate basic cabinets. For instance, the LM8 Light Rail Molding helps conceal under-cabinet lighting, giving your cabinets a custom look.
  7. Reassemble & Adjust:
    Reattach doors and hardware. Make sure everything lines up correctly and that doors swing smoothly. Fine-tune any hinge adjustments for perfect alignment.

Follow these steps carefully to master how to modify RTA cabinets. For further in-depth strategies, consider exploring This Old House’s Kitchen Resources .

6. Maintaining Your Modified RTA Cabinets

After you’ve mastered how to modify RTA cabinets, follow these tips to keep them looking fresh:

  • Regular Cleaning: Use mild soap and water to wipe down surfaces, avoiding harsh chemicals that may damage the finish.
  • Check Hardware: Hinges and pulls can loosen over time. Tighten them periodically for consistent alignment.
  • Protect the Finish: Use felt pads on drawers or cabinet doors to minimize scratches and scuffs.
  • Reapply Topcoat if Needed: High-traffic areas may require a new clear sealant after some years.
